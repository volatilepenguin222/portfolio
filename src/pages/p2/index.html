Webpage: Aleppo Sweets Takeout Menu
At first glance, Aleppo Sweets has a fairly aesthetic website; however, as an interface for placing takeout orders, I found myself looking for missing affordances that would make the ordering process a bit cleaner.

Usability & Accessibility

Problems

  • lack of navigation - no way for a user to refine their search (they have to scroll through the entire menu)
  • to access/view cart contents, a user must scroll up to the beginning of the website (menu does not propogate down as scrolling occurs)
  • manual scroll required - no way to jump back to the top of the page
  • repetitive/uneccessary buttons for items
  • inconsistent spacing between items
  • item images become too large in narrower dimensions
*note: these problems are especially aggravated by the volume of the menu.

Accessibility

  • Contrast errors
    • I actually feel like this webpage does fairly well with text visibility. They choose dark colors against white text, and when they overlay an image with white text, they darken the image accordingly such that there is enough contrast. It may still be something to consider for color-blind users, but I understand the business's desire to portray the liveliness of their products through strategically-placed images.
  • Lack of structural elements
    • There are no header structures nor page regions on this ordering page (which is weirdly inconsistent with other pages of this website). Users of assistive technology often rely on these structures for proper navigation and functionality.
  • Defined language attribute
    • This website is geared towards English speakers and is correctly lang-attributed as so. It's interesting to note that the headers are also listed in Arabic and not attributed as so, but since the Arabic translation is directly under the English header, it's more intuitive and shouldn't pose an issue for the screen readers.
